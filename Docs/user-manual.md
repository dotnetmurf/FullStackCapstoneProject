# SkillSnap User Manual

Welcome to SkillSnap! This guide will help you get started, manage your portfolio, and understand the different types of user access in the application.

---

## 1. What is SkillSnap?
SkillSnap is an online platform for creating and sharing your professional portfolio. You can add your skills, showcase your projects, and manage your profile. Access is available for everyone, but some features require you to log in.

---

## 2. Access Levels

### a. Anonymous (Not Logged In)
- **What you can do:**
  - Browse all public portfolio users
  - View user profiles, projects, and skills
  - See the home page and navigation menu
- **What you cannot do:**
  - Add, edit, or delete any information
  - Access your own portfolio or make changes
  - See admin-only features

### b. Registered User (Logged In)
- **What you can do:**
  - All anonymous features
  - Add new portfolio users (create your profile)
  - Add, edit, or delete your own projects and skills
  - Edit your own profile information
  - Log out securely
- **What you cannot do:**
  - Delete other users or their data
  - Access admin-only features

### c. Admin User
- **What you can do:**
  - All registered user features
  - Delete any portfolio user, project, or skill
  - Access admin-only delete buttons and actions
- **How to become admin:**
  - The default admin account is provided by your organization (see below)

---

## 3. Getting Started

### a. Accessing the Application
1. Open your web browser.
2. Go to the SkillSnap website (ask your administrator for the correct link, e.g., http://localhost:5105 for local testing).

### b. Registering a New Account
1. Click the **Login** button in the top bar.
2. On the login page, click **Register**.
3. Fill in your email and password (password must be at least 6 characters, with uppercase, lowercase, and a digit).
4. Click **Register**.
5. You will be automatically logged in and can now create your portfolio.

### c. Logging In
1. Click the **Login** button in the top bar.
2. Enter your email and password.
3. Click **Login**.
4. You will see your name in the top bar when logged in.

### d. Logging Out
1. Click the **Logout** button in the top bar.
2. You will be returned to the home page as an anonymous user.

---

## 4. Managing Your Portfolio

### a. Creating Your Profile
1. After logging in, click **All Users** in the sidebar.
2. Click **Add Portfolio User** (if available) or select your profile to edit.
3. Fill in your name, bio, and profile image URL.
4. Click **Save**.

### b. Adding Projects
1. Go to your profile page.
2. Click **Add Project**.
3. Enter the project title, description, image URL, and (optionally) a link.
4. Click **Save**. Your project will appear in your profile.

### c. Adding Skills
1. Go to your profile page.
2. Click **Add Skill**.
3. Enter the skill name and select your proficiency level.
4. Click **Save**. Your skill will appear in your profile.

### d. Editing or Deleting Projects/Skills
- Click the **Edit** or **Delete** button next to any project or skill in your profile.
- Confirm deletion when prompted.

## 5. Admin Features

### a. Admin Login
- Use the default admin account:
  - **Email:** admin@skillsnap.com
  - **Password:** Admin123!
- Only admins can delete any user, project, or skill.
- Admin-only delete buttons are visible only to admin users.

---

## 6. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?**
- Contact your administrator. Password reset is not available in this version.

**Q: Why can't I see the Add or Edit buttons?**
- You must be logged in to add or edit your own information.

**Q: Why can't I delete another user's profile?**
- Only admin users can delete any profile. Regular users can only manage their own data.

**Q: How do I become an admin?**
- Only the default admin account is available by default. Contact your administrator for admin access.

---

## 7. Tips & Troubleshooting
- Use a modern browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox) for best results.
- If you see a "Network Error" or can't log in, check your internet connection or contact your administrator.
- For local testing, make sure both the API and Client are running (ask your admin for help).
- Always log out after using a shared computer.
